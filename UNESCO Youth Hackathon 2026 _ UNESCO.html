UNESCO Youth Hackathon 2026

Play Your Part: Youth Designing the Future of Media and Information Literacy (MIL)
UNESCO
10 June 2026
Last update:5 July 2026

The record-breaking 2025 edition, which attracted over 1,200 teams from 138 countries, demonstrated the power of young people to drive innovation and create impactful solutions for today’s rapidly evolving information environment. 

Building in this momentum, the UNESCO Youth Hackathon 2026, under the theme of "Play Your Part: Youth Designing the Future of Media and Information Literacy", recognizes young people as key drivers of innovation and change, empowering them to contribute their creativity, leadership, and diverse perspectives to advance Media and Information Literacy (MIL) worldwide.

The winning teams will be invited by UNESCO to participate in the Voices European Festival of Journalism and Media Freedom in Thessaloniki, Greece, from 26–28 November 2026, where they will have the opportunity to showcase their projects and engage with other stakeholders.

Call for Submission: Win a Trip to Greece

Open to global youth aged 18 – 30 from around the world. Participants can collaborate with their peers and co-design innovative MIL solutions.

 

Submission is now open. We look forward to your projects!

Submit Here
UNESCO

Mentoring Programme

All registered participants will have access to a two-day mentoring programme scheduled for 1 - 2 July featuring experts in Media and Information Literacy (MIL), digital transformation, artificial intelligence, project development, and innovation. These sessions are designed to help you strengthen your ideas and prepare a competitive project proposal.

PS: Live session places are limited and will be allocated on a first-come, first-served basis, so early registration is highly recommended. Participants who do not secure a live place will still receive full access to recordings and learning materials.

How to Prepare

Tips for your preparation

Eligibility

Who Can Participate

UNESCO invites young changemakers to join this global event:

  • Teams of 2–6 members (national, regional, or international)
  • All team members must be 18–30 years old
  • Teams should align with UNESCO’s values, including peace, respect for diversity, and freedom of expression
  • Gender balance and inclusion are highly encouraged
  • No prior experience required—only passion and motivation!
CHALLENGE TRACKS

Where to Start?

Teams can choose one or more of the following focus areas:

  • AI and MIL: Innovative solutions addressing challenges posed by AI through MIL
  • MIL Education: Creative approaches to MIL learning in the digital age
  • Community Impact: MIL-based interventions that empower communities
  • Youth Engagement: Strategies to position youth organizations as MIL change agents
  • Open Track: Other MIL-related ideas aligned with this year’s theme
FORMAT

What Kind of Proposals Are We Looking For?

Your proposal can be one of the following categories:

  • Games
  • Applications/Websites
  • Radio programmes/Podcasts
  • Artistic work such as comics, short videos, documentaries, etc.
  • Educational toolkits
  • Youth organization campaigns
  • Community-based interventions
  • Other creative modalities for innovative interventions
SUBMISSION

How to Submit?

Each team must prepare two components, which can be submitted in English.

Proposal Document (PDF or Word format, size limit: 10MB )

  • Include team members, problem statement, objectives, target audience, prototype or concept, sustainability, creativity, and feasibility
  • Keep it clear, concise, well-structured and readable

Pitch Video (max. 3 minutes)

Create a short video that showcases your project idea in a clear and engaging way. This is your chance to bring your concept to life and convince the jury of its value.

  • Tell the story behind your idea: What problem are you solving, and why does it matter?
  • Explain your solution: What is your project? How does it work? Who will benefit from it?
  • Highlight originality and impact: What makes your idea unique or meaningful? What kind of impact it would have?
EVALUATION

How to Select the Winners

A group of international experts will review all projects during 18 August to 13 September. Each project will be reviewed by 3 experts, to ensure a fair process, based on the criteria below:

  • Consistency with the Theme：strong alignment with this year's theme and MIL principles
  • Clarity of Presentation：clear communication, strong skills, diversity, and potential to deliver
  • Innovation & Creativity：fresh, original, or inventive approach
  • Feasibility & Sustainability：realistic, resource-aware, long-term potential
  • Impact & Inclusion：addresses a real problem, promotes social good, meets the needs of diverse/marginalized communities
WINNERS

What Can Winners Receive?

Four winning teams will be selected at the mid of September. The winning teams will be invited to Voice Festival in Thessaloniki, Greece from November 26-28.

UNESCO will cover 2 members of each team for their flight, accommdation and meal to Greece.
