# DECODE Content Cards — Sample Set (10 Cards)

These are sample Content Cards for the DECODE board game. Each card simulates a real social media post or news headline that players must classify as REAL, MISLEADING, or FAKE.

---

## CARD 01 — 🔴 Theme: Fake News
**[SOCIAL MEDIA POST]**

> 🚨 BREAKING: Vietnamese Ministry of Health confirms new virus spreading through tap water. Boil all water immediately! Share to save lives! 🚨
> 
> Posted by: @HealthAlertVN | 2.3K shares | 45 min ago
> Profile: Created 3 days ago | 12 followers | No bio

**TRUTH CARD #01**: ❌ FAKE
- No official statement from Ministry of Health
- Account created 3 days ago (bot pattern)
- No other news source reporting this
- Uses emotional urgency to force sharing ("Share to save lives!")
- **DECODE Method**: D (urgency language), O (new account, no credentials), C (no other sources)

## CARD 02 — 🟡 Theme: Responsible Media
**[NEWS ARTICLE HEADLINE]**

> "Study Shows Coffee Drinkers Live 15% Longer"
> — HealthDaily.com
>
> A new study published in the Journal of Nutrition found a correlation between moderate coffee consumption (3-4 cups/day) and reduced mortality risk.

**TRUTH CARD #02**: 🟡 MISLEADING
- The study is real (published in actual journal)
- BUT "15% longer" is misleading — the study found correlation, not causation
- The headline oversimplifies a complex finding
- HealthDaily.com is real but sensationalizes
- **DECODE Method**: E (study exists but conclusion exaggerated), D (clickbait headline for ad revenue)

## CARD 03 — 🟢 Theme: Real News
**[NEWS ARTICLE]**

> "UNESCO Launches Global Media Literacy Curriculum for Teachers in 2026"
> — Reuters
>
> UNESCO has released an updated Media and Information Literacy curriculum framework for educators, incorporating AI literacy and digital citizenship modules.

**TRUTH CARD #03**: ✅ REAL
- Published by Reuters (verified news agency)
- UNESCO did release updated MIL curriculum in 2025-2026
- Consistent with UNESCO's known initiatives
- No emotional manipulation in headline
- **DECODE Method**: E (credible source), C (confirmed by UNESCO.org), O (Reuters is established)

## CARD 04 — 🟣 Theme: AI Content
**[IMAGE POST]**

> [Photo of a celebrity at a protest holding a controversial sign]
> 
> Posted by: @TruthRevealed99 | "They don't want you to see this!" | 8.7K shares
> Comments: "OMG is this real??" "I always knew they were..."

**TRUTH CARD #04**: ❌ FAKE (AI Generated)
- Image analysis shows telltale AI signs (hand deformity, background blur inconsistency)
- Celebrity was documented to be in a different country on that date
- Account posts only controversial content
- "They don't want you to see this" is a classic manipulation phrase
- **DECODE Method**: D (conspiracy language), O (anonymous account), E (celebrity's real schedule contradicts)

## CARD 05 — 🟠 Theme: Bot Networks
**[PRODUCT REVIEW]**

> ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ "This supplement changed my life! Lost 10kg in 2 weeks with no exercise. Dr. Nguyen recommends it. Order now before stock runs out!"
> 
> Reviewer: NguyenMinh2026 | Verified Purchase
> 47 other 5-star reviews posted in the same hour

**TRUTH CARD #05**: ❌ FAKE (Coordinated Bot Reviews)
- 47 reviews in the same hour = coordinated campaign
- "Dr. Nguyen" doesn't exist in medical registry
- "Lost 10kg in 2 weeks with no exercise" is medically impossible
- "Before stock runs out" = artificial urgency
- **DECODE Method**: D (too-good-to-be-true claims), O (fake doctor reference), D (selling motive)

## CARD 06 — 🔵 Theme: Filter Bubbles
**[RECOMMENDATION SCREENSHOT]**

> Your TikTok "For You" page showing 10 videos in a row all claiming:
> "The government is hiding THIS from you about [topic]"
> Each from a different account but same script, same background music, same format

**TRUTH CARD #06**: 🟡 MISLEADING (Algorithmic Amplification)
- Individual videos may contain some truth mixed with speculation
- The algorithm amplifies this content because it gets high engagement (clicks, shares)
- Same format across different accounts = coordinated content farming
- Creates a false sense of consensus ("everyone is saying this!")
- **DECODE Method**: C (algorithm shows you more of what you engage with), O (content farm pattern), E (echo chamber effect)

## CARD 07 — 🟢 Theme: Data Privacy
**[APP NOTIFICATION]**

> "🎉 Congratulations! You've won a free iPhone 16! Click here to claim your prize! Just enter your email, phone number, and home address."
> 
> From: SuperPrize_Official | Download link: bit.ly/fr33-ph0n3

**TRUTH CARD #07**: ❌ FAKE (Phishing Scam)
- Classic phishing: "You've won" + asks for personal data
- Suspicious URL (bit.ly with leetspeak: fr33-ph0n3)
- No legitimate company gives away products this way
- Requesting home address = physical security risk
- **DECODE Method**: D (too good to be true), O (unverified account), D (data harvesting motive), E (identity theft risk)

## CARD 08 — 🔴 Theme: Political Disinformation
**[SHARED SCREENSHOT]**

> Screenshot of a "deleted tweet" from a public figure saying something extremely controversial
>
> Caption: "They deleted this but we saved it! RT before they silence us!"
> Shared by 15,000 accounts in 2 hours

**TRUTH CARD #08**: ❌ FAKE (Fabricated Screenshot)
- No archive service (Wayback Machine, archive.today) has this tweet
- Screenshot shows font inconsistencies (wrong tweet spacing)
- "They deleted it" is unfalsifiable — convenient excuse for no proof
- 15,000 shares in 2 hours = likely bot-amplified
- **DECODE Method**: E (no archival evidence), O (who made the screenshot?), C (no independent confirmation), D (political motivation)

---

## CARD 09 — 🟡 Theme: Context Manipulation
**[VIDEO CLIP — 15 seconds]**

> Short clip showing a crowd running in panic at a shopping mall
> 
> Caption: "Terrorist attack at [local mall name] — STAY AWAY!"
> Posted 30 minutes ago | 4.2K shares

**TRUTH CARD #09**: 🟡 MISLEADING (Real Video, False Context)
- The video IS real — but it's from a Black Friday sale rush 3 years ago in a different country
- No reports of any incident at the named mall
- Reverse image search shows the original context
- Caption deliberately creates panic with false framing
- **DECODE Method**: C (no police/news confirmation), O (who posted and why?), E (reverse image search reveals true context)

> 
> Source: VietnamNet, quoting the Government Portal

**TRUTH CARD #10**: ✅ REAL

- Includes verifiable link to decree text
- Consistent with multiple other news sources

- **DECODE Method**: E (official document linked), C (confirmed by multiple outlets), O (government source via reputable news)
