# DECODE Stories — Episode 1: "The Viral Trap"
## Video Script (3-5 minutes)

**Format**: Mini-documentary + animated explainer hybrid
**Tone**: Engaging, slightly urgent, but empowering — NOT preachy
**MIL Competency**: ACCESS — Choosing what to consume and share

### ACT 2: THE DECODE (1:45 - 3:00)

**[VISUAL]**: Screen transitions to clean, animated explainer style

**[NARRATOR - VO]**:
"So what could Minh have done differently? Let's DECODE that post."

**[VISUAL]**: The fake post appears on screen. Each DECODE step highlights a different part.

**[STEP D — DETECT]**
**[VISUAL]**: Red circles appear around "🚨", "!!!", "Chia sẻ ngay!"
**[NARRATOR]**: "D — Detect. Look for red flags. Excessive urgency? Emotional language? 'Share immediately'? These are manipulation tactics, not journalism."

**[STEP E — EVIDENCE]**
**[VISUAL]**: Search bar animation — searching for the claim
**[NARRATOR]**: "E — Evidence. Is there any proof? This post cites no study, no document, no named expert. Zero evidence."

**[STEP C — CROSS-CHECK]**
**[VISUAL]**: Split screen showing 3 news sites — none reporting the story
**[NARRATOR]**: "C — Cross-check. If the Ministry of Health actually warned about tap water, every news outlet would cover it. Reuters, VnExpress, Tuổi Trẻ — silence."

**[STEP O — ORIGIN]**
**[VISUAL]**: Clicking on the profile — animation shows: Created 3 days ago, 12 followers, no photo
**[NARRATOR]**: "O — Origin. Who posted this? An account with no history, no identity, and no credibility. Classic bot pattern."

**[STEP D — DETERMINE MOTIVE]**
**[VISUAL]**: Arrows pointing to potential motives: panic, clicks, political disruption
**[NARRATOR]**: "D — Determine motive. Why would someone create this? Clicks for ad revenue? Political disruption? Chaos for fun? Understanding WHY reveals the truth."

**[STEP E — EVALUATE IMPACT]**
**[VISUAL]**: Showing the consequences that DID happen
**[NARRATOR]**: "E — Evaluate impact. Before you share, ask: If this is wrong, who gets hurt? In this case — thousands of people panicked, hospitals were overwhelmed, real emergencies got ignored."
